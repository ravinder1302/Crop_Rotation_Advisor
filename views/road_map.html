Crop Rotation Road Map

Two-Year Rotation

Alternating between two different crops, typically a legume and a cereal crop. This helps in nitrogen fixation and pest control.

Three-Year Rotation

Rotating between three crops in sequence, often including a root crop, cereal, and legume for balanced soil health.

Four-Year Rotation

Complex rotation system involving four different crops, maximizing soil benefits and minimizing pest and disease risks.

Spring

Plant nitrogen-fixing legumes like peas or beans

Summer

Grow heavy feeding crops like corn or tomatoes

Fall

Plant light feeding crops like root vegetables

Winter

Use cover crops or let soil rest
